Goal and Outcome for EAC Implementation:

A reliable EAC is an approved project cost estimate that reflects the operational labor effort and expenses required to complete the study over time and that can be used confidently as the basis for revenue recognition.

Reaching a reliable EAC: Utilize available EAC process checklists and stay focused on process compliance:

1. EAC first Month

a. Assure that your Baseline matches your EAC for the first submission

b. Submit your EAC and Baseline

2. EAC each Month thereafter

a. Submit your EAC on time

b. Monitor the need to re-baseline

3. Submit your timesheets on time

Review the EAC Project Team dashboard to evaluate your EAC inputs so you can answer questions about your function’s EAC (FLs) and project EAC (PLs)

4. EAC Methodology Checklist

The EAC Methodology Checklist tool provides a set of “best practice” guidelines for project teams to use when updating and reviewing their EAC.

About[edit]

Estimate at Completion (EAC) is a methodology that reports and forecasts actual hours instead of units. Enterprise Project Management (EPM) allows us to improve our project management capabilities and to comply with new international accounting rules for revenue recognition which go into effect January 2018. With new international accounting rules coming into effect, it is imperative that we record and report the actual hours we work and forecast for projects

1.1. Project Expense

Due to a Q2 2017 Security Exchange Commission (SEC) decision, all CROs are required to include both project hours and project expenses in their EAC calculations. Project Expense budget is mapped into 2 different expense types: “Investigator Fees” and “Other Expenses”. Project expense activities are embedded in the standard monthly process. PL/PMAs will review the project expenses “actual” each month and assess whether the total expected final cost needs to be updated (due to changes in final expense). EAC Hours drive the EAC expense forecast.

• PL/Delegate is responsible for submitting EAC for Project expenses on a monthly basis
• PL/Delegate only updates (if needed) the expected final cost and Not to Deliver (NTD) cost.
• Project Expenses phased forecast is based on the EAC hours and is automatically generated.
• PFM must be in agreement on the updates to the expected final cost and NTD cost.
• Though Expected Final Costs and NTD Costs may not need an update, a submission is ALWAYS required
• The EAC Hours must be updated prior to PL/PMA reviewing the EAC Expenses.



1.2 EAC Monthly Process

An EAC and Baseline is required if there is backlog, regardless of the following:

o Work order is not signed yet,
o Budget (QIP/Proposal) is not reflective of what the customer is likely to pursue,
o The work has not started yet, or
o The team is not yet formed

While completing a robust, detailed and accurate EAC and Baseline may be a challenge given the status of the project, the EAC and Baseline should be completed following the defined processes. The following steps may also help:

o Pick a reasonable start date and end date:
o Estimate for each budgeted function (the estimated hours do not need to equal the budgeted hours but the budget should be a key input)
o If you do not have any Functional Leads yet, the PL/PM should complete the estimates for all budgeted functions
o Your estimate can be summarized at 1 work activity level (developing an hourly estimate for every specific line item is not required at this early stage IF it is not yet known/ agreed to with the customer)
o Estimate at the regional level as per the budget
o A monthly estimate is required for 2017 and 2018, but an annual estimate is permitted thereafter
o Consider the use of NTD and OOS where appropriate

Any concerns about the budget loaded or applicability of doing an EAC should be addressed with your PFM

1.3 EAC for Projects on Hold

Projects on Hold: Budget will be loaded into PeopleSoft EAC reflecting hours worked to date as an accurate backlog. Remaining budgeted hours (hours not yet worked) should be forecast into appropriate work activities or placed in “Not to Deliver” in line with the anticipated operational reality. Even while the project is on hold, there is an expectation that the EAC will be reviewed and submitted each month.

1.4 EAC for New Projects

Projects where we have New Wins and/or LOA/LOI/ATP: Budgets should be loaded in accordance with the backlog into PeopleSoft EAC and the Project Manager and Functional Leads should forecast based on the full scope of work as it is currently defined and expected to be completed. When the additional authorization/contract is in place, the team can re-baseline, as required.

Any new projects with financial backlog and a budget must have an EAC and Baseline created and maintained in PeopleSoft. Going forward, EACs will enable budgeting and resource planning – similar to the role revenue and backlog forecasting play today.

1.6 Key Takeaway

• The entire Project Team is responsible for the EAC – if you see an issue, bring it to your team
• PL/PMA can make changes to Functional EACs if necessary, and then apprise the FL
• Use Baseline Version Numbers to correct Baseline errors
• Use the EAC Project Team Dashboard throughout the EAC cycle to view your EAC performance
• Use the Draft WO/CO Sent Report to know when to include OOS Hours into EAC and then Re-Baseline
• FL forecasts should be done on Monday of EAC Week (3rd full week of the month) - the rest of EAC Week is for reviewing and updating the EAC
